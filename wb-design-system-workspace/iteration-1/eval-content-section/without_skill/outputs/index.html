02 // Alert Triage

How we triage alerts

Moving from reactive firefighting to a structured, signal-driven process. The new approach reduces mean time to resolution and eliminates noise before it reaches the on-call engineer.

Before

Manual, reactive triage

  • Every alert pages the on-call engineer regardless of severity or context
  • Runbooks lived in Confluence — outdated, hard to find under pressure
  • No deduplication: related alerts fired separately, flooding Slack channels
  • Post-incident reviews tracked in a spreadsheet, rarely revisited or actioned
After

Automated, signal-driven triage

  • Alerts route through severity rules — P3 and below auto-silence during off-hours
  • Inline runbook links embedded in alert payloads, versioned alongside the service
  • Correlation engine groups related signals into a single incident record
  • Every closed incident writes a structured timeline to the shared knowledge base
