01 // Triage Approach

Before & After

We replaced a reactive, manual triage process with a structured, priority-driven workflow. The shift reduced mean time to acknowledge and eliminated alert fatigue from noise-heavy queues.

Before
  • All alerts land in a shared Slack channel with no priority signal — on-call rotates triage manually
  • Runbooks stored in a mix of Confluence pages, Google Docs, and tribal memory — no single source of truth
  • Severity assigned verbally during incident bridge; no automated classification at ingest time
  • Post-incident reviews scheduled ad hoc, often skipped under delivery pressure — learnings siloed per team
After
  • Alerts enriched at ingest with P1–P4 severity tags; routed to dedicated queues with SLA timers and auto-escalation
  • Every alert links directly to a versioned runbook in the ops repo — reviewed quarterly, owned by the service team
  • ML-assisted deduplication and noise suppression cut weekly alert volume by 60% before on-call sees anything
  • Blameless post-mortems filed within 48h for all P1/P2 events; action items tracked in Jira with weekly review cadence

Signal over noise.

Structure at the edges keeps the center calm.
